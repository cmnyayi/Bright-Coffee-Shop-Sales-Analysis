# Bright-Coffee-Shop-Sales-Analyisis
Conducting Sales Analysis for the CEO to understand the performance of the shop in the last 6 months

### problems statement
The coffee shop has experienced a noticeable decline in sales performance over the past six months. As a data analyst, the goal of this project is to investigate the underlying factors contributing to the drop in revenue and identify patterns or trends that may explain the business underperformance.

### aim
The aim of the project is to analyze transactional data and deliver insights that improve business perfomance

### objectives
- To analyze sales data and identify trends over time
-	To evaluate the performance of different store locations
-	To examine customer purchasing behavior and spending patterns
- To identify peak and low-performing periods (time and day analysis)
- To provide data-driven recommendations to improve revenue

### Tools & Methodology
-	Project Planning: Canva (for presentation and reporting)
-	Brainstorming & Ideation: Miro (for structuring ideas and workflow)
-	Data Processing & Analysis: Databricks (SQL for data cleaning and transformation)
-	Data Visualization: Google Sheets (dashboard and charts)

## Key Analysis

### ☕ Sales by Product Category
The analysis identified the product categories contributing the most to total sales.

This helped determine which categories were the strongest contributors to revenue and which categories may require further attention or promotional support.

###  Sales by Time of Day

The analysis showed that **Rush Hour** was the strongest sales period, while **Night** recorded the lowest sales performance.

This provides an opportunity for the business to:
- Optimise staffing during peak periods
- Ensure sufficient stock availability during high-demand periods
- Introduce promotions during lower-performing periods

### Sales by Day of the Week
Sales performance varied across the days of the week.

The analysis helped identify stronger and weaker sales days, allowing the business to better understand customer purchasing behaviour and plan staffing and promotions accordingly.

### Sales by Month

The monthly analysis showed an overall upward trend in sales towards the later months of the period analysed.

This provides useful information for understanding sales growth and planning future inventory and marketing strategies.

##  Key Insights
- **Top-performing product categories** contributed significantly to overall sales and revenue.
- **Rush Hour** was the strongest sales period, indicating higher customer demand during this time.
- **Night** was the lowest-performing period, creating an opportunity for targeted promotions.
- Sales performance varied across different days of the week.
- Monthly sales showed an overall upward trend towards the later months of the analysis period.
- Customer purchasing behaviour varied depending on the time and day of purchase.
- 
##  Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were made:
### 1. Focus on High-Performing Product Categories
The business should prioritise popular product categories by ensuring consistent stock availability and using them in promotional campaigns.

### 2. Optimise Peak Sales Periods
Additional staff and sufficient inventory should be available during high-demand periods such as Rush Hour.

### 3. Improve Low-Performing Periods
Targeted promotions, discounts, and special offers could be introduced during low-performing periods such as Night.

### 4. Use Sales Trends for Planning
Monthly and daily sales trends should be monitored regularly to support better inventory, staffing, and marketing decisions.

### 5. Monitor Key Performance Indicators
The business should regularly track important metrics such as:
- Total sales
- Sales by product category
- Sales by time of day
- Sales by day of the week
- Monthly sales trends
